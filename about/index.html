About

Closing the EEG education gap in pediatric anesthesia.

PALNET — the Pediatric Anesthesia Learning Network for Emerging Technology — is an international, clinician-led collaborative founded in 2022.

Our mission

To educate and train pediatric anesthesia clinicians on EEG-guided anesthesia — to improve safety, outcomes, and sustainability for patients and the earth.

Our vision

EEG-guided anesthesia will become a standard for anesthetic dosing and neurological monitoring, and a core competency in pediatric anesthesia education and certification.

The problem

FDA-cleared processed-EEG devices for pediatric patients are now installed in operating rooms around the world. Yet most pediatric anesthesia clinicians have never been formally trained to interpret them. The technology arrived years ahead of the education.

Without training, clinicians either ignore the monitor or misread it — and the safety, precision, and sustainability benefits of EEG-guided anesthesia go unrealized. PALNET exists to close that gap.

Our approach

We meet clinicians where they work and learn:

  • Curriculum. Evidence-based modules on raw EEG, the density spectral array (DSA), and the spectral edge frequency (SEF) — built around real pediatric cases.
  • On-site, in-OR teaching. Network teachers spend two to three days at member institutions, teaching at the bedside during live anesthetics.
  • Train-the-trainer. Every visit invests in local faculty so that EEG teaching continues after we leave.
  • Workshops & simulation. Hands-on sessions at international meetings — ASA, CSPA, PAC, and the Safe Brain Initiative — using a purpose-built portable EEG simulator.
  • Online learning. A growing library of recorded lectures and case-based EEG videos clinicians can access on their own time.
  • Mentorship. Post-visit text and video support, plus follow-up meetings every two to three months.

Vendor-agnostic by design

PALNET is supported by both major pediatric EEG vendors (Masimo Sedline and Medtronic BIS Advance), and our curriculum is built to work with any FDA-cleared device that displays raw EEG, DSA, and SEF. We teach the brain, not the brand.

A clinician-led collaborative

PALNET is run by practising pediatric anesthesiologists, by and for the specialty. Our leadership committee spans North America with international teaching partners in Europe, Latin America, and Asia. The Society for Pediatric Anesthesia (SPA) provides administrative support; vendor grants and modest site-visit fees fund operations.

The result is a non-profit education program that is independent, evidence-based, and accountable to the clinicians it serves.

Events & site visits

Where PALNET is teaching next.

PALNET teaches in two main settings — site visits in the operating rooms, and workshops associated with hospitals, universities, and society meetings.

There are two types of site visits: (1) a PALNET teacher goes to a member site and teaches in the operating room “at the elbow” while members deliver anesthesia, or (2) a member goes to a PALNET teacher's hospital and observes “at the elbow” while the teacher delivers anesthesia. Find us at one or invite us to your hospital.

Site visits

Bring PALNET to your hospital.

A PALNET site visit is two days of intensive, in-OR, case-based teaching delivered at your institution — followed by ongoing mentorship to keep your program growing.

1

Before the visit

We work with your team to scope learning goals, identify cases, and prepare the local faculty who will continue teaching after we leave. A pre-visit knowledge assessment establishes a baseline.

2

During the visit

PALNET teachers spend two days in your operating rooms teaching with you as you deliver anesthesia, supplemented by didactic sessions and practice with our EEG simulator.

3

Train-the-trainer

Every visit invests in local faculty. We work shoulder-to-shoulder with the colleagues who will sustain EEG teaching at your institution, giving them the curriculum, the case library, and the confidence to keep going.

4

After the visit

A post-visit knowledge assessment, ongoing text and video support, and follow-up meetings every two to three months for as long as your team finds them useful.

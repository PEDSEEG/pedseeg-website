Pediatric anesthesia · EEG-guided care

Bringing EEG-guided anesthesia to every operating room — for every child.

PALNET is an international, clinician-led network training pediatric anesthesia teams to use EEG for safer, more precise, and more sustainable anesthetic care.

What PALNET does

Processed-EEG devices are now in pediatric operating rooms worldwide, but most clinicians have never been trained to read them. PALNET closes that gap with a complete educational program — curriculum, in-OR teaching, simulators, and long-term mentorship.

Why EEG?

Anesthesia titrated to the brain, not the body.

Anesthetic depth varies dramatically between a six-month-old and a teenager — but standard dosing has long relied on age and weight rather than what the brain is actually doing. EEG-guided anesthesia gives clinicians a direct window into cortical activity, helping prevent both under- and over-dosing, reducing intraoperative drug use, and shortening recovery.

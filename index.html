Pediatric anesthesia · EEG-guided care

Bringing EEG-guided anesthesia to every operating room — for every child.

PALNET is an international, clinician-led educational organization for EEG-guided pediatric anesthesia — enabling better safety, better outcomes, more precise dosing, and more sustainable perioperative care.

What PALNET does

EEG devices are now in many pediatric operating rooms worldwide, but most clinicians have never been educated and trained to use them. PALNET closes that gap with a complete educational program — didactic curriculum, in-OR teaching, workshops, and long-term mentorship.

①

Didactic Curriculum

Review articles on EEG, pediatric EEG, anesthetic effects on pediatric EEG, and EEG-guided TIVA; research papers providing evidence for clinical practice; smartphone “look-up” tables for use in the OR; and lectures.

②

In-OR teaching

PALNET teachers — certified experts in EEG-guided pediatric anesthesia — visit your institution for two days of “at-the-elbow” teaching during anesthesia care, bringing the education to real experience in your practice. PALNET uses a “train-the-trainer” model, focusing on a few individuals during the site visit so they develop the confidence to grow their skills and teach their colleagues. PALNET members may also visit a PALNET teacher to spend a few days “at the elbow” while the teacher performs EEG-guided anesthetics.

Why EEG?

Anesthesia titrated to the brain, not the body.

Anesthetic depth varies dramatically between a six-month-old and a teenager — but standard dosing has long relied on age and weight rather than what the brain is actually doing. EEG-guided anesthesia gives clinicians a direct window into cortical activity, helping prevent both under- and over-dosing, reducing intraoperative drug use, and shortening recovery.

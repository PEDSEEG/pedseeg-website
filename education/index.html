The PALNET curriculum.

A curated video curriculum and primary literature on EEG-guided pediatric anesthesia — free to view, designed for clinicians who want to start reading the brain in the operating room today.

Video curriculum

Our recommended starting point is the EEG for Anesthesia YouTube channel — a concise, evidence-based introduction to the EEG signal and how anesthetic drugs shape it. PALNET's in-OR teaching and workshops build directly on this foundation.

About the channel

Current standards for assessing depth of anesthesia rely on indirect measures — heart rate, blood pressure, muscle tone, and presumed drug interactions. Modern systems-level neuroscience now lets us link recognizable EEG signatures to the anesthetic states produced by most commonly used drugs. Familiarity with these signatures helps anesthesia clinicians make more detailed and accurate assessments of the anesthetic state of their patients. These videos provide a brief, accessible introduction to the EEG signal as it relates to altered states of arousal caused by anesthesia.

Suggested essential reading — pending vetting

Ten papers that may serve as a foundation for clinicians beginning to learn EEG-guided pediatric anesthesia. To be reconciled with PALNET's canonical reading list.

  1. Foundational

    General Anesthesia, Sleep, and Coma

    Brown EN, Lydic R, Schiff ND · New England Journal of Medicine · 2010;363(27):2638–2650

    The foundational synthesis of how anesthetics produce unconsciousness, situating anesthetic states alongside natural sleep and pathologic coma. The mental model everything else in this list builds on.

    DOI

  2. Foundational

    Clinical Electroencephalography for Anesthesiologists: Part I — Background and Basic Signatures

    Purdon PL, Sampson A, Pavone KJ, Brown EN · Anesthesiology · 2015;123(4):937–960

    The single most-cited reference in this field. Walks the practising anesthesiologist through raw EEG, the density spectral array (DSA), and the characteristic signatures of each anesthetic drug class — exactly the vocabulary PALNET teaches in workshops.

    DOI

  3. Foundational

    Clinical Electroencephalography for Anesthesiologists and Intensivists: Part 2 — Physiologic Signatures and Active Management

    Guay J, Agrawal M, et al. · Anesthesiology · 2025

    The follow-up to Purdon's 2015 reference, focused on active EEG management in the operating room and ICU. The "how do I act on what I see?" counterpart to Part I.

    DOI

  4. PALNET author Pediatric Open access

    Intraoperative pediatric electroencephalography monitoring: an updated review

    Yuan I, Bong CL, Chao JY · Korean Journal of Anesthesiology · 2024;77(3):289–305

    PALNET officer Ian Yuan's comprehensive update on pediatric intraoperative EEG monitoring — neurodevelopmental EEG changes, non-proprietary parameters for dosing, epileptiform changes during induction, and how EEG translates to clinical outcomes. The most current pediatric review.

    DOI

  5. Pediatric

    A Narrative Review Illustrating the Clinical Utility of Electroencephalogram-Guided Anesthesia Care in Children

    Bong CL, Balanza GA, Khoo CEH, et al. · Anesthesia & Analgesia · 2023

    A narrative review illustrating, with annotated EEG examples, when and how EEG-guided practice changes pediatric anesthesia management. Useful for clinicians who want to see concrete case-based reasoning rather than theory.

    DOI

  6. Pediatric Foundational Open access

    Age-dependent electroencephalogram (EEG) patterns during sevoflurane general anesthesia in infants

    Cornelissen L, Kim S-E, Purdon PL, Brown EN, Berde CB · eLife · 2015;4:e06513

    Seminal demonstration that the pediatric EEG under sevoflurane has fundamentally different age-dependent signatures than the adult EEG. The empirical foundation for everything PALNET teaches about developmental EEG.

    DOI

  7. PALNET author Pediatric

    Using Electroencephalography (EEG) to Guide Propofol and Sevoflurane Dosing in Pediatric Anesthesia

    Yuan I, Xu T, Skowno J · Anesthesiology Clinics · 2020;38(3):709–725

    The most clinician-friendly practical guide we recommend: how to actually titrate propofol and sevoflurane using the EEG you have in front of you. Written by a PALNET officer.

    DOI

  8. PALNET author Implementation

    Implementation of an electroencephalogram-guided propofol anesthesia education program in an academic pediatric anesthesia department

    Yuan I, Missett RM, et al. · Pediatric Anesthesia · 2022

    First major implementation paper documenting how an academic pediatric anesthesia department rolled out an EEG-guided propofol education program. PALNET's site-visit and train-the-trainer model builds directly on this work.

    DOI

  9. PALNET partner Implementation Open access

    Introducing the Safe Brain Initiative's EEG boot camp for anaesthesia for standardised training on how to use the electroencephalogram for perioperative care

    von Dincklage F, Helfrich K, Koch S, et al. · BMC Anesthesiology · 2025

    Describes the standardized perioperative EEG training model developed by PALNET's collaborator, the Safe Brain Initiative. Provides much of the framework PALNET site visits and workshops draw on.

    DOI

  10. PALNET author Pediatric

    EEG-Guided Pediatric Anesthesia — A Quality Innovation?

    Kurth CD · JAMA Pediatrics · 2025

    PALNET founder C. Dean Kurth's editorial framing EEG-guided pediatric anesthesia as a quality innovation worth structured implementation. The clinical-quality argument behind PALNET's existence.

    DOI
